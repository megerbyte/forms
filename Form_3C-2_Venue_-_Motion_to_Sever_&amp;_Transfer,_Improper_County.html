C. Argument & Authorities

8. On the issue of venue, plaintiff has the burden to offer prima facie proof, and defendant is not permitted to offer rebuttal evidence. See Surgitek, Bristol-Myers Corp. v. Abel, 997 S.W.2d 598, 602 (Tex. 1999).

9. On the issue of joinder, Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code places the initial burden on plaintiff to offer prima facie proof of the four joinder elements listed in Section 15.003(a). Surgitek, 997 S.W.2d at 602-03. On this issue, defendant may offer rebuttal evidence. Id. at 603. If defendant’s joinder evidence rebuts plaintiff’s prima facie proof, the trial court may consider all available evidence to resolve the dispute. Id. Thus, in a hearing for a Section 15.003(a) joinder determination, the trial court can permit more evidence than it would in a venue hearing. Surgitek, 997 S.W.2d at 603.
