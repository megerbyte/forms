C. Legislative Continuance

30. While the Legislature is in session, the trial court must grant a motion for legislative continuance if either the attorney or the party is unavailable because the attorney or party is a member or member-elect of the Legislature. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §30.003(b); Tex. R. Civ. P. 254. When a party makes a proper motion for legislative continuance, a continuance is mandatory, unless the other party can show irreparable harm by the delay. See In re Ford Motor Co., 165 S.W.3d 315, 319 (Tex. 2005); Waites v. Sondock, 561 S.W.2d 772, 776 (Tex. 1977); In re Starr Produce Co., 988 S.W.2d 808, 811 (Tex. App.—San Antonio 1999, orig. proceeding). {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Continuance for Legislator,” ch. 5-D, §12, p. 262.}
