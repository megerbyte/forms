C. Argument & Authorities

5. The due process clauses of both the Texas and the United States Constitutions guarantee a party an impartial and disinterested tribunal in civil cases. Marshall v. Jerrico, Inc., 446 U.S. 238, 242 (1980); Metzger v. Sebek, 892 S.W.2d 20, 37-38 (Tex. App.—Houston [1st Dist.] 1994, writ denied).

6. {Party} will be deprived of a fair trial in violation of the due process clauses of both the state and federal constitutions and Texas Rule of Civil Procedure 18b because

{CHOOSE APPROPRIATE GROUNDS}

(A) the judge’s impartiality might reasonably be questioned. Tex. R. Civ. P. 18b(2)(a); Woodruff v. Wright, 51 S.W.3d 727, 735-36 (Tex. App.—Texarkana 2001, pet. denied). The inquiry the court must make is whether a reasonable member of the public, knowing all the facts in the public domain, would have a reasonable doubt that a judge is actually impartial. Sears v. Olivarez, 28 S.W.3d 611, 613 (Tex. App.—Corpus Christi 2000, no pet.); see also Woodruff, 51 S.W.3d at 736 (reasonable person standard). {See O’Connor’s Texas Appeals, “Impartiality might be questioned,” ch. 13-J, §4.1.1, p. 109.}

(B) the judge has a personal bias or prejudice against {the subject matter of the case/a party}. Tex. R. Civ. P. 18b(2)(b); Wright v. Wright, 867 S.W.2d 807, 811 (Tex. App.—El Paso 1993, writ denied); see, e.g., Aetna Life Ins. Co. v. Lavoie, 475 U.S. 813, 821-22 (1986) (judge was biased because success of judge’s own suit before lower court depended on ruling in case before judge). The judge’s bias is extra-judicial and is not based on in-court rulings. In re M.C.M., 57 S.W.3d 27, 33 (Tex. App.— Houston [1st Dist.] 2001, pet. denied). {See O’Connor’s Texas Appeals, “Bias & prejudice,” ch. 13-J, §4.1.2, p. 109.}

(C) the judge has personal knowledge of a disputed fact in this suit. Tex. R. Civ. P. 18b(2)(b). {See O’Connor’s Texas Appeals, “Judge has personal knowledge,” ch. 13-J, §4.1.3, p. 110.}

(D) {the judge/an attorney with whom the judge previously practiced law} has been a material witness concerning this proceeding. Tex. R. Civ. P. 18b(2)(c). {Identify proceeding and describe role as witness.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Appeals, “Material witness,” ch. 13-J, §4.1.4, p. 110.}

(E) the judge, while acting as an attorney in government service, {participated as counsel, adviser, or material witness in/expressed an opinion regarding the merits of} the controversy. Tex. R. Civ. P. 18b(2)(d). {See O’Connor’s Texas Appeals, “Judge expressed an opinion,” ch. 13-J, §4.1.5, p. 110.}

(F) the judge knows that {he/she}, as {an individual/a fiduciary}, has {a financial interest in the subject matter in controversy/a financial interest in a party to the proceeding/{identify other interest}}, which could be substantially affected by the outcome of the proceedings.
