No party shall attempt to compel {name of impartial third party}’s testimony, to compel {name of impartial third party} to produce any documents provided by any party, or to compel any party to testify regarding statements made in the mediation sessions, unless the oral communications or written materials are admissible or discoverable independent of the alternate dispute resolution procedure. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Confidentiality,” ch. 4-A, §7, p. 218.}

5. {Name of impartial third party} shall not disclose confidential information provided during the mediation on behalf of any party, testify on behalf of any party, or submit any type of report to the court in connection with this suit, other than to state whether the parties reached a settlement. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Confidentiality,” ch. 4-A, §7, p. 218.}

6. The mediation shall be confidential, privileged from discovery, and otherwise conducted in accordance with Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code section 154.073. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Confidentiality,” ch. 4-A, §7, p. 218.}
