D. Continuance for {Additional Discovery/Unavailable Witness}

11. A court can grant a continuance for additional testimony only if the motion for continuance includes the following: (1) a description of the testimony needed, (2) the name and residence of the person from whom the testimony is sought, (3) the materiality of the testimony, (4) a description of the diligence used to secure the evidence, and (5) the cause, if known, of the failure to secure the testimony earlier. Tex. R. Civ. P. 252; see State v. Wood Oil Distrib., Inc., 751 S.W.2d 863, 865 (Tex. 1988); J.E.M. v. Fidelity & Cas. Co., 928 S.W.2d 668, 676 (Tex. App.—Houston [1st Dist.] 1996, no writ); Gabaldon v. General Motors Corp., 876 S.W.2d 367, 370 (Tex. App.—El Paso 1993, no writ). {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Continuance for Additional Discovery,” ch. 5-D, §8, p. 256; “Continuance When Witness or Party Unavailable,” ch. 5-D, §10, p. 260.}
