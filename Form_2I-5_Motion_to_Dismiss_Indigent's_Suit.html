{Suit filed by indigent who is not an inmate}

{Suit filed by indigent who is an inmate}

7. The court has the authority to dismiss a suit filed by an inmate if the allegation of poverty in the {affidavit/unsworn declaration} is false. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §14.003(a)(1). {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Not indigent,” ch. 2-I, §5.4.1, p. 142.}

7. The court has the authority to dismiss a suit filed by an inmate if the action is frivolous or malicious because the claim has no arguable basis in fact. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §14.003(a)(2), (b)(2). {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “No arguable basis,” ch. 2-I, §5.4.2, p. 142.}

7. The court has the authority to dismiss a suit filed by an inmate if the action is frivolous or malicious because the claim has no arguable basis in law. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §14.003(a)(2), (b)(2). {Elaborate.} No hearing is necessary for the court to determine this issue. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Hearing,” ch. 2-I, §5.1, p. 142; “No arguable basis,” ch. 2-I, §5.4.2, p. 142.}

7. The court has the authority to dismiss a suit filed by an inmate if the inmate filed an {affidavit/unsworn declaration} relating to previous filings that the inmate knew was false. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §14.003(a)(3); see id. §14.004. {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Inmate - false filing,” ch. 2-I, §5.4.3, p. 142.}

7. The court has the authority to dismiss a suit filed by an inmate if the action is frivolous or malicious because the claim is substantially similar to a previous claim filed by the inmate that was based on the same operative facts. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §14.003(a)(2), (b)(4). {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Inmate - substantially similar claim,” ch. 2-I, §5.4.4, p. 142.}

7. The court has the authority to dismiss a suit filed by an inmate if the action is filed more than 30 days after the date the inmate receives a written decision from the grievance system. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §14.005(b). {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Inmate - untimely claim,” ch. 2-I, §5.4.5, p. 142.}
