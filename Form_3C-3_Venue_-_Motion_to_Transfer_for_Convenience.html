Form 3C:3 Venue - Motion to Transfer for Convenience

{STYLE OF THE CASE - FORM 1B:2}

DEFENDANT’S MOTION TO TRANSFER FOR CONVENIENCE

Defendant files this motion to transfer venue and asks the court to transfer the case from {current} County to {proposed} County.

A. Introduction

1. Plaintiff is {name}; defendant is {name}.

2. Plaintiff sued defendant in {current} County for {state basis of suit}.

3. This case should be transferred out of {current} County to {proposed} County for the convenience of the parties and witnesses and in the interest of justice. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Venue convenient elsewhere,” ch. 3-C, §2.3.5, p. 163.}

B. Facts

4. {State facts relevant to the motion.}

5. Defendant attaches affidavits to this motion to establish defendant’s venue facts and incorporates them by reference. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Affidavit,” ch. 3-C, §2.3.5(2), p. 164.}

C. Argument & Authorities

6. Defendant requests that the court transfer this case to {proposed} County, under the authority of Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code section 15.002(b).

7. Maintaining this suit in {current} County will impose an economic and personal hardship on defendant. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §15.002(b)(1). Specifically, {state why suit in current county will impose an economic and personal hardship and why suit in proposed county would not}. {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Hardship on defendant,” ch. 3-C, §2.3.5(1)(a), p. 164.}

8. The balance of interests of all parties weighs in favor of the action being brought in {proposed} County. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §15.002(b)(2). Specifically, {list factors that weigh in favor of proposed county}. {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Balance of interests,” ch. 3-C, §2.3.5(1)(b), p. 164.}

9. The transfer to {proposed} County will not work a hardship or an injustice on any other party. Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §15.002(b)(3). Specifically, {state specific reasons why the move would not impose a hardship or injustice on plaintiff and any other parties}. {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “No hardship on plaintiff,” ch. 3-C, §2.3.5(1)(c), p. 164.}

10. {Current} County is not a county of mandatory venue, and {proposed} County is a county of proper venue. See Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §15.002(b). {Elaborate.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Proper county,” ch. 3-C, §2.3.5(1)(d), p. 164.}

D. Conclusion

11. Because venue is more convenient in {proposed} County, a county of proper venue, the court should transfer this case to {proposed} County.

E. Prayer

12. For these reasons, defendant asks the court to set {his/her/its} motion to transfer venue for hearing and, after the hearing, grant defendant’s motion and transfer this case to {proposed} County. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Request hearing,” ch. 3-C, §2.2.5, p. 163.}
