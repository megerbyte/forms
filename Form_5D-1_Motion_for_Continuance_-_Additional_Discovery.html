Form 5D:1 Motion for Continuance - Additional Discovery

{STYLE OF THE CASE - FORM 1B:2}

{PARTY}’S MOTION FOR CONTINUANCE

{Party} asks the court to continue this case for {number} days because additional time is needed to obtain {testimony/evidence} in this case. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Continuance for Additional Discovery,” ch. 5-D, §8, p. 256.}

A. Introduction

1. Plaintiff is {name}; defendant is {name}.

2. Plaintiff sued defendant for {state basis of suit}.

3. Discovery in this suit is governed by a Level {1/2/3} discovery control plan. The discovery period {will end/ended} on {date}. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Discovery Control Plans,” ch. 6-A, §7, p. 332.}

4. {All/Most/A significant part/No significant part/None} of the discovery has been completed.

5. This case is set for {trial/hearing} on {date}.

B. Facts

6. {Party} has not made an unconditional announcement of ready for trial.

7. {State other facts relevant to the motion.}

8. {Party} attaches affidavits to this motion to establish facts not apparent from the record and incorporates them by reference.

C. Argument & Authorities

9. The court may grant a continuance to obtain additional testimony when the motion for continuance includes the following: (1) a description of the testimony needed, (2) the identification and residence of the person from whom the testimony is sought, (3) the materiality of the testimony, (4) a description of the diligence used to secure the evidence, and (5) the cause, if known, of the failure to secure the testimony earlier. Tex. R. Civ. P. 252; see State v. Wood Oil Distrib., Inc., 751 S.W.2d 863, 865 (Tex. 1988); Gabaldon v. General Motors Corp., 876 S.W.2d 367, 370 (Tex. App.—El Paso 1993, no writ); Verkin v. Southwest Ctr. One, Ltd.,784 S.W.2d 92, 94-95 (Tex. App.—Houston [1st Dist.] 1989, writ denied). {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Continuance for Additional Discovery,” ch. 5-D, §8, p. 256.}

10. {Party} needs additional time to secure {testimony/evidence} regarding {describe the testimony or other evidence needed}.

11. The {testimony/evidence} is material to {party}’s {claim/defense} of {identify claim or defense} because {state facts showing materiality of evidence}.

12. {Party} will be prejudiced if unable to obtain the {testimony/evidence}. {Explain.}

13. {Party} was unable to secure this {testimony/evidence} earlier even though {he/she/it} diligently used the discovery process. {Summarize discovery attempts demonstrating diligence. If known, state why discovery attempts did not secure evidence.}

{ADD APPROPRIATE PARAGRAPHS 14-19}

{If continuance based on need for deposition}

14. This testimony can be secured by the deposition of {name of witness}, who resides at {street address, county, state}.

15. This witness has been unavailable for deposition because {state facts showing unavailability}.

16. {Party} expects the deposition of {name of witness} to provide the following testimony: {describe expected testimony}.

17. {Party} sent a notice of intent to take the deposition of {name of witness}. The notice is attached as Exhibit {letter}.

{If continuance based on need for other discovery}

18. To secure this evidence, {party} needs additional time to {describe type of discovery needed, e.g., request production of documents}. Attached to this motion as Exhibit {letter} is the {describe discovery request, e.g., request for production of documents}.

{If second or later motion}

19. This is {party}’s {number} motion for continuance. Thus, {party} must show the testimony sought by this motion cannot be obtained from any other source. Tex. R. Civ. P. 252. {State facts that demonstrate inability to obtain testimony elsewhere.} {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Not otherwise available,” ch. 5-D, §8.7, p. 257.}

D. Conclusion

20. {Briefly summarize the motion.}

21. This request for continuance is not for delay only, but so that justice may be done.

E. Prayer

22. For these reasons, {party} asks the court to continue this case for at least {number} days until {date}.
