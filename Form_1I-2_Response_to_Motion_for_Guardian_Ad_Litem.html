C. Argument & Authorities

7. {Party} recognizes that the court must appoint a guardian ad litem to protect the interests of a party when required by Texas Rule of Civil Procedure 173. However, Rule 173 does not require the appointment of a guardian ad litem in this case.

{CHOOSE APPROPRIATE PARAGRAPHS 8-11}

8. {Adverse party} alleged there was an adverse interest between {name of incapacitated party} and {name of guardian or next friend}, {his/her} {guardian/next friend} in the division of settlement proceeds. When there is no adverse interest, the appointment of a guardian ad litem is not necessary. See Tex. R. Civ. P. 173.2(a)(1); see, e.g., Kennedy v. Missouri Pac. R.R., 778 S.W.2d 552, 555 (Tex. App.—Beaumont 1989, writ denied) (trial court properly denied motion to appoint guardian ad litem; there was no indication next friend sought to compromise minor’s recovery for her own benefit). Because there is no adverse interest between the party and the {guardian/next friend}, the court should not appoint a guardian ad litem. {Elaborate.}

9. {Adverse party} alleged that there is an agreement to appoint a guardian ad litem for {name of incapacitated party}. The parties have not agreed to appoint a guardian ad litem in this case. See Tex. R. Civ. P. 173.2(a)(2). {Elaborate.} Therefore, the court should not appoint a guardian ad litem.

10. {Adverse party} alleged that a guardian ad litem is necessary to determine whether {name of incapacitated party}’s {guardian/next friend} has an adverse interest to {name of incapacitated party} that should be considered by the court under Texas Rule of Civil Procedure 44. The court may appoint a guardian ad litem in this role only in extraordinary circumstances. Tex. R. Civ. P. 173 cmt. 4. This case is not an extraordinary circumstance because {explain}. Therefore, the court should not appoint a guardian ad litem.

11. Rule 173 requires the appointment of the same guardian ad litem for all similarly situated parties who are represented by a guardian or next friend, unless the court finds the appointment of a different guardian ad litem is necessary. Tex. R. Civ. P. 173.2(b). {The parties are not similarly situated/Even though the parties are similarly situated, different guardians ad litem are necessary} in this case because {explain}. Therefore, if the court grants {adverse party}’s motion to appoint a guardian ad litem, the court should appoint different guardians ad litem for the parties.
