Form 6J:1 Motion to Enter Property of Nonparty

{STYLE OF THE CASE - FORM 1B:2}

{PARTY}’S MOTION TO ENTER PROPERTY OF NONPARTY

{Party} asks the court to order {name of nonparty} to permit {party} to enter the property located at {address}, as allowed by Texas Rule of Civil Procedure 196.7. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Motion,” ch. 6-J, §4.1, p. 455.}

A. Introduction

1. Plaintiff is {name}; defendant is {name}; nonparty is {name of nonparty}.

2. Plaintiff sued defendant for {state basis of suit}.

3. Discovery in this suit is governed by a Level {1/2/3} discovery control plan. The discovery period {will end/ended} on {date}. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Discovery Control Plans,” ch. 6-A, §7, p. 332.}

4. This case is set for {trial/hearing} on {date}.

B. Facts

5. The property subject to this motion is owned by {name of nonparty}, {a person/an entity} who is not a party to this suit, whose address is {address}, and whose telephone number is {number}.

6. As a result of the suit, it is necessary for {party} to enter the property located at {address}, and owned by {name of nonparty}, for the purpose of {inspecting/ measuring/surveying/photographing/testing/sampling} the property.

7. This motion is served more than 30 days before the end of the discovery period.

8. {State other facts relevant to the motion.}

9. {Party} attaches affidavits to this motion to establish facts not apparent from the record and incorporates them by reference.

C. Argument & Authorities

10. The court may issue an order for entry on a nonparty’s property if the movant shows good cause and that the property or an object on the property is relevant to the subject matter of the lawsuit. Tex. R. Civ. P. 196.7(d); In re Swepi L.P., 103 S.W.3d 578, 583 (Tex. App.—San Antonio 2003, orig. proceeding).

11. There is good cause for the court to allow {party} to enter on {name of nonparty}’s property because {specify facts supporting good cause}.

12. {Party}’s entry on {name of nonparty}’s property is relevant to this lawsuit because {explain why entry is relevant or is likely to lead to the discovery of relevant evidence}.

D. Time, Place, & Other Conditions

13. The order on a motion to enter the property of another must include the time, place, manner, conditions, and scope of the inspection, and describe the desired means, manner, and procedure for testing or sampling, and the person or persons by whom the inspection, testing, or sampling is to be made. Tex. R. Civ. P. 196.7(b).

14. {Party} suggests that the inspection take place on {date}, at {time}, or at any other time agreeable to the parties and {name of nonparty}.

15. {Party} requests permission to {inspect/measure/survey/photograph/test/ sample} {the property/{identify item on the property}}. {Elaborate.}

16. The scope of the {inspection/measurement/survey/photographing/testing/ sampling} will be {identify scope}.

17. The person who will conduct the {inspection/measurement/survey/ photographing/testing/sampling} is {name of person}, who is qualified to perform the {inspection/measurement/survey/photographing/testing/sampling} by {his/her} training and education. {Elaborate.}

18. {Party} asks the court to allow {identify other persons who will attend, e.g., attorney} to attend.

{IF TESTING OR SAMPLING, ADD PARAGRAPH 19}

19. The proposed means for {testing/sampling} is {identify the specific means for testing or sampling}. The {testing/sampling} will not destroy or materially alter the item to be {tested/sampled}. Tex. R. Civ. P. 196.5. {Elaborate, if necessary.}

E. Conclusion

20. {Briefly summarize the motion.}

F. Prayer

21. For these reasons, {party} asks the court to set {his/her/its} motion for hearing and, after the hearing, order {name of nonparty} to allow {party} to enter {his/her/its} property.
