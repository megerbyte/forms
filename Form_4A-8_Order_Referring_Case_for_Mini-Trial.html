ORDER ON REFERRAL FOR MINI-TRIAL

After considering the parties’ agreed motion to refer the case for a mini-trial under Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code section 154.024, the court

GRANTS the agreed motion and orders as follows:

1. This case is referred for a mini-trial under Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code section 154.024. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Mini-trial,” ch. 4-A, §2.2.1, p. 215; “Order for ADR,” ch. 4-A, §5, p. 217.}

2. The proceedings in this case shall be stayed pending the outcome of the mini-trial.

3. The parties shall conduct the mini-trial before

{CHOOSE ONE OF THE FOLLOWING}

(A) selected representatives for each party. The parties must select their representatives and submit the names to the court by {date}.

(B) {name of impartial third party}, who is the impartial third party selected by the parties.

4. The {selected representatives/impartial third party} will assist the parties in defining the issues and developing a basis for realistic settlement negotiations.

5. The mini-trial will be conducted according to the following terms and conditions: {state the date, time, and place for the mini-trial}. All proceedings related to the mini-trial must be concluded by {date}.

6. The following parties and their counsel shall attend the mini-trial and remain present until its completion: {list parties and their attorneys}.

7. All statements made during mini-trial sessions and all materials prepared for the mini-trial are confidential. Any record made during the mini-trial is confidential. No party shall attempt to compel any party to testify regarding statements made in the mini-trial or to compel any party to produce any documents provided by any party during the mini-trial, unless the oral communication or written materials are admissible or discoverable independent of the alternate dispute resolution procedure. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Confidentiality,” ch. 4-A, §7, p. 218.}

8. The mini-trial shall be confidential, privileged from discovery, and conducted in accordance with Texas Civil Practice & Remedies Code section 154.073. {See O’Connor’s Texas Rules, “Confidentiality,” ch. 4-A, §7, p. 218.}

9. If the parties enter into a written settlement agreement following the mini-trial, the agreement will be binding.
